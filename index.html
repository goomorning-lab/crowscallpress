Questions

Frequently asked questions.

Who are these workbooks designed for?
Students in Grades 1–6+ with varying reading levels. Crown emphasizes comprehension and guided learning; Leaders develops independent, critical thinkers. Levels align with ability, not just grade.
What makes these different from regular workbooks?
Every page has been crafted on years of classroom experience in Korea's rigorous education system. The result is a workbook that is visually engaging, carefully scaffolded, and structured to take students from comprehension to critical analysis.
Do teachers need prior experience?
No. We provide step-by-step training and PPTs for guided writing so any motivated teacher can run a high-quality program. Partner schools receive ongoing professional development.
Can we trial the program before committing?
Yes, please contact us directly so we can provide material specific to your program's interests.
What essay types do students learn?
Students progress through Opinion Essays, Compare & Contrast Essays, and Hamburger Essays, each building analytical skills. Advanced levels add interpretive theses and critical analysis.
What support do partner schools receive?
All plans include full lesson plans, answer keys, vocabulary sheets, and guided writing PPTs. Growth and Scale tiers include professional development.

Who We Are

Built on curiosity.
Shaped by craft.

Crow's Call Press takes its name from an old story about a crow that lifted the water in a pitcher, pebble by pebble, until it could finally drink. We see learning in the same way: a creative, deliberate act that raises understanding one idea at a time. Founded by a team of American educators now based in Busan, South Korea, we bring together backgrounds in literature, art, and debate to reimagine what English education can be. Our programs and publications combine storytelling, analysis, and communication, helping students find voice and meaning in what they learn. We believe education is both an art and a craft, shaped by curiosity, empathy, and the willingness to look closer. Every lesson we create begins with a simple call to think, to imagine, and to reach a little higher.

Full Curriculum

The Reading List

A complete year of curated literature for each level with one book per month, spanning 12 genres. Click any book to find it on Kyobo or Aladin.

Civic Level

Step Into Reading: Step 4. Focus on fluency, vocabulary-in-context, and literal comprehension. CEFR A1–A2.

Candidate Level

Magic Tree House, Junie B. Jones, and A to Z Mysteries series. Focus on summarizing chapters, character & setting tracking, and predictions. CEFR A1–A2.

Monarch Level

Bridges Crown and Leaders. Focus on genre familiarity, theme spotting, and text-to-self connections. CEFR A2–B1.

Junior Level

High-level elementary readers ready for gentle literary analysis. Claim + Evidence basics, literary elements, discussion norms. CEFR A2–B1.

Lexis Level

Middle-school-level texts; handles complex themes & structure. Author craft, figurative language, cross-text connections. CEFR B1–B2.

Mythos Level

Strong, independent readers. Interpretive theses, rhetoric, genre conventions. Sustained close reading & annotation. CEFR B2–C1.

Opsis Level

Exceptionally advanced; college-style discussion & writing. Critical lenses, comparative analysis, synthesis writing. CEFR C1+.
